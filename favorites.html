Sarah's Favorites

The Best of the Best

My Brilliant Friend

Elena Ferrante

Ghosts

Dolly Alderton

The Ocean at the End of the Lane

Neil Gaiman

Set in a poor neighborhood in Naples, Italy, this novel follows the lives of two girls, Elena and Lila, as they navigate the complexities of friendship, family, and social mobility, against the backdrop of political turmoil and cultural change in postwar Italy. Through its richly drawn characters and vivid portrayal of a particular time and place, My Brilliant Friend offers a poignant and thought- provoking exploration of the human experience.

This coming-of-age novel follows the life of Nina Dean, a thirty-something woman living in London, as she navigates the ups and downs of modern dating, relationships, and friendships, all while grappling with the ghosts of her past and the uncertainties of her future. With its relatable characters and witty prose, Ghosts offers a humorous and insightful look at the challenges of adulthood and the search for love and fulfillment.

The Ocean at the End of the Lane by Neil Gaiman is a mesmerizing novel that blends fantasy and memory. The story follows an unnamed protagonist who, upon returning to his childhood home, recalls a series of magical and unsettling events involving a mysterious girl named Lettie Hempstock and the otherworldly wonders at the edge of an ordinary pond.

Throne of Glass

Sarah J. Maas

I'm Glad My Mom Died

Jennette McCurdy

The Days of Abandonment

Elena Ferrante

After serving time as a convict, teenage assassin Celaena Sardothien is given a chance to win her freedom by participating in a deadly competition to become the king's champion, but she soon finds herself embroiled in a dangerous game of politics and intrigue. This young adult fantasy novel is full of action, romance, and plot twists that will keep readers on the edge of their seats.

This memoir provides a candid and often humorous look at Jennette McCurdy's experiences as a child actor, as well as her struggles with eating disorders and her complicated relationship with her mother, who was both a source of love and abuse. Through her writing, McCurdy offers a unique and unflinching look at the dark side of Hollywood and the toll it can take on young actors.

When her husband of 15 years suddenly leaves her, Olga is left to grapple with the shock, pain, and confusion of abandonment, while also caring for her two young children and confronting her own inner demons. This intense and emotional novel by Elena Ferrante explores the raw and messy realities of love, loss, and the complexities of the human psyche.
